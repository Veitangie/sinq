# Contributing to Sinq

Thank you for considering contributing! Before you dive into the codebase, please read this document carefully. This project operates with some strict legal and operational boundaries to ensure its long-term survival.

## The Philosophy & The CLA

I strongly believe in open-source, and the community version of this project will always remain GPLv3. However, I prefer the existence of money over the lack of it, so I'd like to keep the door open for commercial dual-licensing in the future. 

To make that legally possible, I need the necessary rights to re-license the entire codebase. This is why you will need to sign a Contributor License Agreement (CLA) to contribute to this particular repository. 

**The Guarantee:** The CLA you sign includes an explicit, legally binding "Outbound License Guarantee" which ensures that your contributions will *always* remain publicly available under the GPLv3. I may distribute a proprietary version at some later time, but it will always be accompanied by the main official GPLv3-licensed distribution.

If you don't want to sign the CLA, you are always welcome to fork the project and never contribute back. That's the beauty of open-source!

> *(Note: A bot will automatically prompt you to sign my CLA deriving from the Apache ICLA via your GitHub account on your first Pull Request).*

## Anti-AI Policy

**Do not submit AI-generated code to this repository.** To maintain the ability to dual-license this project, the maintainer must hold clear, undisputed rights to distribute, relicense, and sublicense the codebase. Under current US Copyright Office rulings, code generated by LLMs (like GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) is generally not eligible for copyright protection.

By submitting a Pull Request, you represent that:
1. The code was written entirely by a human (you).
2. You did not use AI tools to generate the logic or boilerplate.
3. You have the legal right to grant the licensing rights of this code to the project.

I reserve the right to reject Pull Requests whose provenance cannot be reasonably established, including those that appear to contain AI-generated code.

## The Workflow: Fork and Pull

All changes submitted to the project are reviewed through Pull Requests; direct pushes are disabled.

To contribute:
1. **Fork** the repository to your own GitHub account.
2. **Clone** your fork locally.
3. **Branch** off `main` for your feature or bug fix (`git checkout -b feature/my-cool-thing`).
4. **Commit** your changes and push them to your fork.
5. **Open a Pull Request** against the upstream `main` branch.

## Expectations & Governance

Your time is valuable. To avoid wasting it:
* **Open an Issue First:** If you are planning a massive architectural change, a new feature, or anything beyond a simple bug fix/typo correction, please open an Issue to discuss it first. 
* As the project maintainer, I retain the right to reject technically sound Pull Requests if they do not align with the project's core vision, architecture, or scope. Discussing it beforehand ensures we are on the same page!

Contributions of all sizes are appreciated, including:

* Bug fixes
* Documentation improvements
* Tests
* Performance improvements
* New features (after discussion)

Again, thank you for your time, your code, and your understanding of the project's goals.
