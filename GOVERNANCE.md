# Governance

The `sinq` project is governed under a model colloquially known as **Benevolent Dictator for Life (BDFL)**. 

Currently, the sole maintainer and BDFL of this project is me - Veitangie.

## The BDFL Model

Unlike consensus-based open-source projects (which often rely on committees, voting, or decentralized ownership), `sinq` is driven by a single unified vision. This model has proven itself pretty well in many projects already, and it requires the least resources of all. Thus, as a project in its early stages, `sinq` is likely to benefit from this choice.

### What this means for contributors:

* **Vision and Roadmap:** I own the roadmap and have the absolute final say on the technical direction, architecture, and feature set of the project.
* **Pull Requests:** Contributions are highly appreciated. However, I retain the right to reject pull requests that do not align with the project's core philosophy, even if the code itself is of high quality.
* **Prior Discussion:** To ensure your time is respected, please open an Issue to discuss massive architectural changes or new features *before* writing any code.

## Dispute Resolution

In the event of disagreements over API design, architectural choices, or implementation details, my decision is final. There is no voting process or appeals committee.

If you fundamentally disagree with the direction of the project or a rejected feature, you are always encouraged to exercise your rights under the GPLv3 license: fork the repository and manage your own version the way that aligns with your own vision.

## Code of Conduct

This governance model does not mean hostility. I will do my best to provide polite, constructive, and clear feedback on PRs and Issues. In return, contributors are expected to keep discussions technical, professional, and focused on the code. But really just don't be an ass and it'll all be fine.

> If this tool ever gains traction and resources, the governance model **might** be revised in the future.
